The Basics of Recording Music

Recording Space

Studio

Studios are designed with sound in mind, so they are the ideal space to record in. From large open rooms to small intimate spaces, every studio is different, thus producing unique recordings.

Home

If you do not have the resources to book studio time, recording in the home is an option. You can rent equipment from an outside company to produce the recordings in your home. Recording in your home allows you to work at your own pace as you are not being billed hourly.

Other

Any space with great acousics can be considered for recording. Large spaces such as churches, can create unique recordings with natural reverb.

Microphones

Condenser

Condenser microphones are more sensitive and require an outside power source or battery. Condenser microphones have a greater frequency response and transient response. They are more sensitive to loud sounds, but generally have a louder output.

Dynamic

Dynamic microphones are more durable than condenser microphones and often less expensive. They are able to handle louder sound pressure levels, making them ideal for loud sound sources.

Outboard Gear

Compressors

Compression helps level the loudness throughout a piece. It makes the quiter parts louder and louder parts quiter for a more even listen. It can also be used as an effect to help bring forward a specific sound source in the mix.

Equalizers

Equalizers allow you to adjust the tone of the different elements in the recording. Equalizers adjust the amplitude of a specific frequency of a sound source.

Effects

Effects such as reverb, echo, and phaser can be added to the sound sources in a recording to help add depth and space to the mix.
